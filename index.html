Section 01

The Generic Bank Product Effectiveness Model (GBPEM)

1.1 — Model Philosophy and Purpose

The Generic Bank Product Effectiveness Model (GBPEM) was conceived to solve one of the most persistent and costly failures in rural banking technology adoption: the blind replication of large commercial bank products in cooperative banking institutions that lack the infrastructure, staff capability, client digital readiness, and financial capacity to absorb them. India's 352 DCCBs have, for two decades, watched large banks deploy superapp platforms, AI underwriting engines, and sophisticated treasury systems — and then attempted to procure similar solutions, often resulting in failed implementations, vendor lock-in, and investments that delivered a fraction of their promised returns.

The GBPEM is a structured, five-axis analytical instrument that allows SSPL, NABARD, and DCCB management boards to make evidence-based decisions about which banking products to implement, in what sequence, and through which delivery mechanism. It replaces aspiration-driven procurement with data-driven prioritisation. The model draws from field diagnostics across Maharashtra, Telangana, Odisha, UP, Gujarat, and Northeast India.

1.3 — How SSPL Uses the GBPEM

SSPL — Sahakar Sarathi Pvt Ltd — is NABARD's shared services entity backed by the Ministry of Cooperation and NCDC, mandated to serve 130 million cooperative banking customers and targeting ₹1,500 crore in annual savings from Year 5 of full operation. The GBPEM is SSPL's primary diagnostic instrument, deployed before any product recommendation is made.

SSPL's advisory model operates in three layers: (a) Regulatory Compliance Layer — CBS stabilisation, CRAR/SLR automation, and Aadhaar eKYC as non-negotiables; (b) Operational Efficiency Layer — compliance engines, NPA EWS, MIS dashboards, and NACH collection; (c) Customer Value Layer — digital products sequenced from highest-adaptability (AePS, UPI, RuPay) to highest-complexity (AI credit scoring, WhatsApp Banking, e-NWR lending).

Product 14 · Internal Operations
🚨 NPA Early Warning System (EWS) — The Banking Pulse Monitor

The EWS reads subtle signals weeks before a default occurs, so managers can intervene as advisors rather than debt collectors. Built on Marginal PD — the correct IFRS 9 ECL metric — it deliberately avoids Conditional PD which overestimates risk by 10–15%, unnecessarily blocking capital. ECL = PD × LGD × EAD (LGD ~40–60% for secured agri loans).

APYAtal Pension Yojana — pension scheme for unorganised sector workers aged 18–40; DCCB earns ₹120 enrollment fee from PFRDA
BBPSBharat Bill Payment System — NPCI interoperable bill collection; DCCB/BC earns ₹3–5 commission per transaction; self-sustaining PACS incentive
BCBusiness Correspondent — last-mile banking agent; micro-ATM device; earns ₹10,000–18,000/month at active transaction volume
BharatNetGovernment initiative for high-speed broadband to all gram panchayats; Phase 2 critical for Zone 3 DCCBs' customer-facing digital ROI
CASACurrent Account Savings Account — core CBS module for retail account lifecycle; SHG group account management; PACS member tracking
CBSCore Banking Solution — centralised banking software; all cooperative banks now CBS-compliant as of March 2025 per RBI mandate
C-EdgeTCS + SBI joint venture; purpose-built CBS for RRBs and cooperative banks; most widely deployed cooperative-sector CBS in India
CRARCapital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio = (Tier1 + Tier2) / RWA × 100; minimum 9% for cooperative banks (RBI mandate)
CTRCash Transaction Report — mandatory FIU-IND filing for cash transactions exceeding ₹10 lakhs; auto-generated in DM-RFI compliance engine
CVDSClient Value Delivery Score — GBPEM Axis 5; Score 5 = directly solves DCCB farmer/SHG member pain point; Score 1 = urban-only product
DCCBDistrict Central Cooperative Bank — 352 DCCBs as of March 2025; middle tier of STCCS; apex credit institution at district level
DBTDirect Benefit Transfer — government digital payment of subsidies directly to Jan Dhan accounts; key Segment C banking product
DM-RFIDigitally Mature Rural Financial Institution — DCCB Category 1; ~55–70 DCCBs; SSPL posture: "Optimise and AI-integrate"
DMSDocument Management System — digital repository replacing flood-prone physical loan file rooms; Tier 1 (Google Workspace) to Tier 3 (AI-DMS)
EADExposure at Default — outstanding loan balance at time of default; component of ECL = PD × LGD × EAD under IFRS 9
ECLExpected Credit Loss — IFRS 9 provisioning metric: ECL = PD × LGD × EAD; requires Marginal PD (not Conditional PD) for accuracy
ENSURENABARD Supervisory Returns Portal — online portal for cooperative bank regulatory data submissions; target for CBS API direct integration
e-NWRElectronic Negotiable Warehouse Receipt — WDRA-issued digital receipt enabling post-harvest commodity lending at 70–80% LTV without distress selling
EWSEarly Warning System — automated CBS monitoring flagging pre-NPA accounts; DM-RFI target: 60%+ of eventual NPAs flagged 30+ days early
FIQFinancial Impact Quotient — GBPEM Axis 4; Score 5 = >50% cost reduction or significant revenue uplift; break-even <18 months
FIU-INDFinancial Intelligence Unit India — central agency receiving CTR and STR filings from all banks and regulated financial entities
FPOFarmer Producer Organisation — legally registered collective of farmers; highest-ROI DCCB client segment for supply chain finance (₹60L/year)
GBPEMGeneric Bank Product Effectiveness Model — 5-axis framework (PMS/RAS/ICI/FIQ/CVDS) for evidence-based RFI product decisions
HRMSHuman Resources Management System — cloud SaaS options: GreytHR (₹50–80/employee), Zoho, Keka, Spine; deploy Month 1 for all categories
ICIImplementation Complexity Index — GBPEM Axis 3; Score 1 = 18+ months, ₹5 crore+; Score 5 = <90 days, <₹25 lakhs, no IT staff needed
IFRS 9International Financial Reporting Standard 9 — accounting standard mandating Expected Credit Loss provisioning; effective April 2024
IVRInteractive Voice Response — automated phone menu in regional language; works on all mobile phones; zero literacy required; Segment C product
KCCKisan Credit Card — revolving crop credit product for farmers; anchor product of DCCBs; digital LOS reduces TAT by 70%; highest FIQ product
LGDLoss Given Default — proportion of loan balance lost on default; ~40–60% for secured agricultural loans; ECL component
LOSLoan Origination System — digital workflow managing application-to-disbursal; KCC LOS has highest FIQ (5) and CVDS (5) of all 20 products
MFAMulti-Factor Authentication — biometric + OTP; mandated by RBI for all banking systems accessing customer accounts
MISManagement Information System — analytics converting CBS data to intelligence; Tier 1 (Google Sheets, free) to Tier 3 (SSPL AI analytics, ₹5–15L/yr)
MVDSMinimum Viable Digital Stack — irreducible minimum digital products per RFI category: 20 components (DM) → 12 (PD) → 6 (PDC)
NABARDNational Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development — apex development bank; SSPL parent; ₹42,000+ crore infused to cooperative banks FY25
NACHNational Automated Clearing House — NPCI mandate-based debit; requires seasonal variable-date adaptation for KCC agricultural repayment patterns
NCDCNational Cooperative Development Corporation — government cooperative development agency; co-owner of SSPL with NABARD and Ministry of Cooperation
NDTLNet Demand and Time Liabilities — denominator for SLR (18%) and CRR (4.5%) computation; auto-computed from CASA CBS module in compliance engine
NDVINormalised Difference Vegetation Index — satellite crop health metric (ISRO NRSC); DM-RFI AI credit scoring input; EWS orange/red zone trigger
NPANon-Performing Asset — loan with principal/interest unpaid for 90+ days (90 DPD); classified: sub-standard (provisioned 15%), doubtful, loss
NPCINational Payments Corporation of India — operates UPI, IMPS, NACH, AePS, BBPS, RuPay, BHIM, UPI 123PAY, USSD *99# banking
OSSOff-Site Surveillance System — NABARD regulatory monitoring; 16 standard returns; 780 hours manual → 90 hours target via compliance engine
PACSPrimary Agricultural Credit Society — village-level cooperative; 63,000 functional; last-mile credit delivery; BBPS PACS generates ₹2.88L/year income
PDProbability of Default — use Marginal PD (not Conditional PD) for IFRS 9 ECL to avoid 10–15% risk overestimation and capital blockage
PDC-RFIPre-Digital Commencing RFI — DCCB Category 3; ~85–90 DCCBs; CBS minimal/unstable; SSPL posture: "Foundation First"
PD-RFIPartially Digitalised RFI — DCCB Category 2; ~195–210 DCCBs; CBS live but incomplete; SSPL posture: "Complete and Consolidate"
PMFBYPradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana — crop insurance; government subsidises 85%+ of premium; auto-enrolment target 95%+ at KCC disbursal (DM-RFI)
PMJDYPradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana — financial inclusion scheme; 450M+ accounts as of 2024; all with RuPay debit card; gateway to digital ecosystem
PMSProduct Maturity Score — GBPEM Axis 1; Score 5 = proven at 100M+ scale (YONO, UPI); Score 1 = experimental stage only
RASRural Adaptability Score — GBPEM Axis 2; Score 5 = works on 2G USSD, BC-agent compatible, no smartphone; Score 1 = urban-only product
RuPayNPCI domestic card network; India's alternative to Visa/Mastercard; zero annual fee for PMJDY accounts; gateway to digital ecosystem
SHGSelf-Help Group — 10–20 member savings/credit collective; group digital literacy makes individual digital adoption viable for Segment B women
SIEMSecurity Information and Event Management — platform aggregating and analysing security logs; PD-RFI standard cybersecurity requirement
SLRStatutory Liquidity Ratio — 18.00% minimum of NDTL in approved securities; fortnightly; CBS investment module auto-alerts at 18.50% threshold
SOCSecurity Operations Centre — 24×7 cybersecurity monitoring; ₹50–100L standalone vs ₹3–8L per DCCB via SSPL shared SOC
SSPLSahakar Sarathi Pvt Ltd — NABARD shared services entity; 130M customers; ₹1,500 crore savings target Year 5; domains: Technology/Operations/Support
StCBState Cooperative Bank — state-level apex of STCCS; 34 StCBs as of March 2025; above DCCBs in the cooperative credit hierarchy
STRSuspicious Transaction Report — mandatory FIU-IND filing for unusual patterns; auto-generated in DM-RFI AI AML system
TAPNABARD Turn Around Plan — rehabilitation for 96 weak RCBs (C/D category) from April 2025; covers technology, governance, HR, business
UPIUnified Payments Interface — NPCI real-time payments; 10 billion+ monthly transactions (2024); gateway to entire digital financial ecosystem
USSDUnstructured Supplementary Service Data — *99# feature-phone banking without internet; works on all GSM mobiles; zero data balance required
V-CIPVideo Customer Identification Process — RBI-approved remote KYC via live video call; enables zero-branch account opening; not yet in cooperative sector
VAPTVulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing — RBI-mandated annual security testing for all banks including cooperative banks
WDRAWarehousing Development and Regulatory Authority — issues e-NWRs; regulates warehouses; enables post-harvest commodity lending product
